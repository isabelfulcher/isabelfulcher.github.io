I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Harvard Data Science Initiative. I work with Bethany Hedt-Gauthier to evaluate and optimize the implementation of digital maternal health interventions in Rwanda and Tanzania.

I am primarily interested in the use of innovative data science tools to improve the delivery of sexual and reproductive health care to at-risk populations. My statistical interests include the development of causal inference methods that account for unmeasured confounding or peer effects on a network.

Recently, I have been engaged in multiple COVID-19 related projects. My team and I have developed a data processing and analysis pipeline to conduct syndromic surveillance in Liberia, Mexico, Malawi, Lesotho, Sierra Leone, Haiti, and Rwanda using routinely collected data. I am also developing sampling methods for seroprevalence studies among healthcare workers in low resource settings. Read more about our team's ongoing work.
